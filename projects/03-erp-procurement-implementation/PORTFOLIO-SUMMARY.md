# ERP Procurement Request-to-Purchase Order Implementation Case

## Positioning Statement

A practical Business Analyst portfolio case showing how a new Business Administration graduate can support a platform-neutral ERP-style procurement workflow project from discovery through requirements, UAT, reporting, and pilot rollout planning.

## Business Problem

The fictional company, HarbourPoint Marine Services, uses email and spreadsheets to manage procurement requests for maintenance parts, tools, safety equipment, vendor repairs, and operations supplies. This creates inconsistent request intake, unclear approval status, missing budget codes, manual procurement follow-up, weak receiving visibility, and limited reporting for managers.

## What I Built

I built a complete synthetic implementation-support artifact pack for a Maintenance department procurement request-to-PO pilot. The project documents the current process, defines a future ERP-style workflow, translates business needs into requirements and user stories, prepares UAT materials, connects sample workflow data to SQL and dashboard requirements, and outlines rollout, training, support, cutover, and risk planning.

This is not a real ERP implementation. It is a portfolio case designed to demonstrate practical BA and implementation analyst thinking.

## Key Artifacts Created

- Executive summary for the business problem, future state, expected benefits, risks, and pilot success metrics.
- Discovery materials, including stakeholder interview guide, scope, assumptions, dependencies, risks, and open questions.
- Current-state and future-state procurement process maps.
- Business requirements document, functional requirements, non-functional requirements, business rules catalogue, approval matrix, and traceability matrix.
- Agile-style user stories with acceptance criteria.
- Synthetic procurement request dataset, data dictionary, SQL query pack, and Power BI-style dashboard design specification.
- UAT test cases and defect log template.
- Rollout note, cutover checklist, training/support note, and implementation risk register.

## Tools Represented

- **Excel / CSV:** approval matrix, traceability matrix, UAT cases, defect log, risk register, and synthetic procurement dataset.
- **SQL:** reporting queries for backlog, approvals, exceptions, spend, vendor delays, and receiving status.
- **Power BI:** dashboard specification with KPIs, pages, filters, visuals, and business-use notes.
- **Process mapping:** current-state and future-state workflow maps using Mermaid-style process documentation.
- **Jira / Confluence-style documentation:** user stories, acceptance criteria, requirements IDs, test IDs, defect IDs, decision notes, and traceability.

## Role Relevance

This project is relevant to **junior Business Analyst** roles because it shows discovery, process mapping, requirements, business rules, stakeholder framing, and traceability.

It is relevant to **Implementation Analyst** roles because it connects workflow requirements to UAT, rollout planning, training, cutover, support, and pilot adoption.

It is relevant to **Business Systems Analyst** roles because it translates business needs into system-facing concepts such as roles, statuses, validations, approval routing, audit history, reporting extracts, and access expectations.

It is relevant to **Process Analyst** roles because it compares email/spreadsheet current-state work with a standardized future-state workflow and identifies pain points, controls, handoffs, and exceptions.

It is relevant to **Operations Analyst** roles because it focuses on practical operational visibility: request backlog, approval aging, budget-code issues, vendor delays, receiving status, and category spend.

## Interview Walkthrough Path

1. Start with the executive summary to explain the business problem and why email/spreadsheet procurement creates operational gaps.
2. Open the current-state and future-state process maps to show how the workflow changes from informal intake to controlled request-to-PO handling.
3. Review the business requirements, business rules, approval matrix, and traceability matrix to show how business needs become testable requirements.
4. Walk through the user stories and UAT cases to show how requirements connect to acceptance criteria and pilot validation.
5. Open the synthetic dataset, SQL queries, and dashboard specification to explain how reporting would support approval follow-up, exception management, spend review, and receiving visibility.
6. Finish with the rollout note, cutover checklist, training/support note, and risk register to show implementation readiness and change support.
